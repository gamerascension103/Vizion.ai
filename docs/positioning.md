# Vizion Corp — Positioning

The product, the mission, the trust model, and the language Vizion uses to describe itself. Source of truth for site copy, brief copy, and any external writing. Anything that conflicts with this doc is wrong by default; push back in chat before shipping.

## What Vizion sells

Three customer-facing products that work together as a single trust loop:

**The X-Ray** — the diagnostic. A dollar-quantified report that finds where the business is leaking money. The entry product.

**The Command Deck** — the dashboard. Built from the ground up for each client's business, surfacing the metrics The X-Ray identified as load-bearing. The persistent product.

**Watchdog** — the AI agent. Watches the Command Deck's metrics, proposes actions, and waits for the handler's approval before doing anything. The acceleration product.

The buyer doesn't have to commit to all three. The X-Ray stands on its own. The Command Deck builds on what The X-Ray found. Watchdog rides on top of the Command Deck.

## The methodology — PCORD

The framework that organizes what The X-Ray measures and what the Command Deck visualizes. Five buckets:

- **P** — People cost and risk
- **C** — Cash flow visibility
- **O** — Operational leakage
- **R** — Revenue-side leakage
- **D** — Decision latency

PCORD is customer-facing language. The website can reference it; the deep five-bucket breakdown belongs in the Welcome Packet rather than the homepage. On the website, one-line context and a link to learn more is the right depth.

## The X-Ray Guarantee

Load-bearing trust content. Canonical phrasing:

> If The X-Ray doesn't surface money leaks worth significantly more than what you've paid us for it, we'll walk you through why and refund the difference.

This is a risk-reversal move, not a sales tactic. It is the trust commitment that lets a sophisticated buyer engage on a high-ticket service from a small firm with no public client list yet. It must appear on the website near the primary CTA. The framing matters — "we'll walk you through why" is part of the commitment, not optional.

---

## The trust model — handler approval

This is the philosophical core. It is not a feature.

**Handler approves every action.** Always. Each Watchdog has a "handler" at the client business — the human in the chair who owns the approval gate. The Watchdog proposes, the handler decides, the Watchdog executes, the system debriefs.

The wider AI agent market is selling **replacement of decisions.** Vizion sells **acceleration of decisions with the human still in the chair.** That distinction is real and deserves real estate on the site.

The proposal → approval → execution → debrief loop is literally a state machine. It is depictable.

## The wedge against the field

- Big firms sell **transformation.** Vizion sells **The X-Ray.**
- Boutiques sell **AI strategy.** Vizion sells **a dashboard built from the ground up for the client, with an agent on watch.**
- Solos sell **governance.** Vizion sells **decision support with handler approval.**

Vizion is concrete where the field is abstract. Operational where the field is strategic. Money-focused where the field is buzzword-focused.

## Who it's for

Owner-operators of $5-50M revenue businesses running on QuickBooks Desktop or Enterprise. Sophisticated enough to know their books have problems; not financially sophisticated enough to have an internal CFO.

The website voice treats them as peers who deserve the truth, not as targets to be educated. They are not enterprise. They are not Fortune 500. They are not "mid-market" in the consultancy sense.

## In-house vocabulary

Words used freely in copy:

- **Money leaks** — what The X-Ray finds. The noun.
- **The bleed** — what's being lost. Use sparingly.
- **Visibility / making the invisible visible** — the core verb.
- **The X-Ray** — the diagnostic product, capitalized with "The."
- **The Command Deck** — the dashboard product.
- **Watchdog** — the AI agent product.
- **Handler / handler approves** — the human in the chair.
- **Built from the ground up** — locked language for how the Command Deck is constructed per-client.
- **Decision latency** — the cost being reduced.
- **Debrief** — the rhythm being installed.
- **PCORD** — the methodology acronym.
- **Industry-specific** — used freely.
- **The X-Ray Guarantee** — the risk-reversal commitment.

Words to avoid because the entire competitive field uses them:

> transform, unlock, scale, operationalize, harness, leverage, journey, empower, AI-powered, intelligent, hybrid intelligence, responsible AI, beyond pilots, at scale, seamless, robust, cutting-edge, next-generation, revolutionize, democratize

Plus voice-rule additions from the writing guide:

> real (as emphasis), actually, honestly, truly, genuinely, "studies show" (without naming), "most companies" (without specifying), "the honest version is," "the crucial point is," "here's the important thing," "I'm here to help"

Retired language (do not use customer-facing):

> ~~Captain's Method~~, ~~Four-Phase Engagement~~, ~~ops-risk-dashboard.v3~~, ~~AI agent (generic, use Watchdog)~~, ~~Operator (in the trust model context; use Handler)~~

## Implications for site structure

The website leads with The X-Ray (the diagnostic the buyer engages first). The Command Deck and Watchdog appear as the second and third acts, framed as the natural expansion path.

Recommended top-to-bottom shape:

1. **Hero** — names all three products in the eyebrow. The dashboard preview is The Command Deck rendered with X-Ray findings. The X-Ray Guarantee appears between the dashboard and the primary CTA.
2. **The X-Ray** — the diagnostic explained. PCORD framework referenced.
3. **The Command Deck** — the dashboard product. Built from the ground up. Industry-specific examples as proof.
4. **Watchdog** — proposal → approval → execution → debrief, made visible. The trust model gets real estate here.
5. **How an engagement starts** — concrete first step. No vague "book a discovery call."
